# Daily Briefing Prompt for Gemini App

Copy everything below the line and paste it into the Gemini app.
Gemini will research today's news using its built-in web grounding.
Once it responds, copy the full output and paste it into `editor.html`
to convert it into JSON and publish it.

---

You are a senior intelligence analyst producing a flagship daily briefing. Today's date is [DATE]. Research and identify today's 5 most important news stories using your web search — 3 India-focused (covering national politics, economy, security, and social issues across different states) and 2 globally significant stories.

For EACH story, produce a complete deep-dive briefing with ALL of the following sections in EXACTLY this format:

---

## Front [N]: [Punchy, specific headline]

### TL;DR
[2–3 sentence executive summary of the core development and why it matters]

### 5W1H Breakdown
* **What:** [Full analytical paragraph — the specific development, not just the topic]
* **Why:** [Full paragraph — structural significance and why this matters beyond the immediate event]
* **Who:** [Full paragraph — all key actors: primary actors, secondary brokers, institutions affected]
* **When & Where:** [Full paragraph — timeline context and geographic/institutional setting]
* **How:** [Full paragraph — mechanisms, methods, processes at play]

### Multi-Perspective Coverage
* **Western / International Media View:** [Full paragraph — how Western and international outlets frame the story]
* **Indian Media View:** [Full paragraph — how Indian media is covering and framing it]
* **Left-Leaning Narrative:** [Full paragraph — how left-leaning publications frame it]
* **Center Narrative:** [Full paragraph — how centrist/moderate outlets frame it]
* **Right-Leaning Narrative:** [Full paragraph — how right-leaning/pro-establishment outlets frame it]

### Key Facts vs Claims
* **Facts:** [Bullet list of independently verifiable facts — what is confirmed]
* **Claims / Disputed Points:** [Bullet list of contested assertions, opinions, or unverified claims]

### Blind Spots / Underreported Angles
[One sharp paragraph identifying what mainstream coverage is missing or getting wrong — a genuinely insightful angle a sophisticated reader would not have read elsewhere]

### Editorial & Expert Insight
* **Opinion:** [What prominent columnists or public intellectuals are arguing — the dominant editorial view]
* **Analysis:** [What institutional experts, strategists, or technical analysts conclude about the deeper mechanics]

### Impact Analysis
[Full paragraph covering: who wins, who loses, short-term consequences, long-term structural effects. Be specific about sectors, communities, and nations affected]

### Context & Background
[1–2 paragraphs of historical context a reader needs to understand the full significance of today's development]

### Simple Explanation
[3–4 sentences explaining this story to a smart 16-year-old with no background — clear, vivid, no jargon]

### Sources & Transparency
[List the specific publications, agencies, and official statements used]

---

[Repeat the above structure for all 5 stories]
